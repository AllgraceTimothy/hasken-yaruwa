The Challenge We Face

Understanding the barriers to girls' education in Nigeria

Problem Statement

In Nigeria, women and young girls face a significant challenge in accessing quality education, which limits their chances of gaining economic independence and possible leadership positions in the future.

This problem primarily affects those living in rural areas with limited access to educational facilities, as well as those who face cultural, religious, or financial barriers which prevent them from pursuing higher education.

This is a significant issue because, through education, women gain greater control over their lives and in turn a wider access to jobs, and so the overall state of economic development improves, and gender inequality diminishes. Failure to address the issue may lead to continued poverty cycles and underrepresentation of females in leadership and skilled professions.

Our Holistic Solution

A comprehensive approach to removing barriers and creating sustainable change

"Our solution is to create a unified support program/platform designed to increase girls' independence and reduce gender inequality through a foundation. We plan to provide rural girls with the financial aid, mentorship, transportation support, and parental engagement they need to stay in school, focusing on removing the everyday barriers the girls face."

Scholarship Program

Designed to bridge the financial gaps that generally keep girls out of school. By providing fully funded academic support which enables girls to enrol, this allows them to compete on an equal footing with their peers.

  • Transportation support

  • Uniforms and necessary supplies

Community Benefits

The entire community benefits from this solution. The young girls benefit from receiving education, and the potential to earn money from a career in the future. This money they earn can be used to support their families back home and economically grow their communities.

"Education is a right each girl deserves. The disadvantage of the community they were born into is not a good enough reason for them to lack this right."
